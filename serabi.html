Serabi Cake

Serabi cake ( Sd. Sorabi, surabi ; Jw. Srabi ) is a traditional market snack originating from Indonesia. There are two types of pancakes, namely sweet pancakes that use kinca (liquid brown sugar) and salted pancakes with or without sprinkling oncom that has been seasoned on it. In Bandung , pancakes are usually sold in the morning and are cooked using a stove to produce a distinctive taste. Sometimes a chicken eggwhich has been shaken is added to the pancake mixture that is being cooked. Along with the times, many continue to innovate by adding various toppings such as sausages , cheese , and mayonnaise that aim to break the assumption that pancake is a food that looks low. Places that serve pancakes with a variety of flavors are spread in big cities like Jakarta , Bandung and Bogor .
