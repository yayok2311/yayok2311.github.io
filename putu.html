Putu Cake

Putu cake is a traditional type of eating of the archipelago in the form of cakes filled with palm sugar , wrapped in grated coconut , and coarse-grained rice flour . This cake is steamed by placing it in a slightly compacted bamboo tube . This cake is sold at sunset until late at night. The distinctive sound of steam coming out of the whistling tool is also a promotional tool for traders who sell.
Most of the colors of this Putu cake are white and green.
A number of traders today replace bamboo with PVC pipes for practical reasons, although in terms of health the use of PVC pipes is harmful to health.
Putu Bugis version (South Sulawesi) uses black glutinous rice without sugar. Putu is eaten with a sprinkling of grated coconut and chili sauce. Putu Bugis is only sold in the morning as a practical breakfast substitute.
