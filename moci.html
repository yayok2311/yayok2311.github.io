Mochi Cake

Mochi ( Japanese : 餅; Hanzi : (麻 糬)) is a Japanese cake made from glutinous rice, pounded so soft and sticky, then shaped into a round. In Japan, this cake is often made and eaten during traditional mochitsuki celebrations or Japanese new year celebrations . However, this type of cake is sold and can be obtained at cake shops throughout the year. It has a distinctive taste that is soft when first eaten, and eventually becomes sticky.
In Indonesia, especially mochi cakes made by Sukabumi City which are commonly found on the street of Cassowary and Ahmad Yani Street, and are often sold by hawkers at several intersections of major roads in the city ​​of Bogor , Mochi Cake ingredients are easy to obtain, made from sticky rice in it contains peanut mixture.
Packed in bamboo baskets branded in Kuo-i written as swang sie, which means a lot of happiness, each mochi basket usually contains 10 mochi sized marbles , with prices starting from Rp 5,000 to Rp 7,500 per share containing four bamboo baskets. In Sukabumi itself, the famous Mochi Cake is Mochi Cake made on Jalan Kaswari . This Mochi cake is round, studded with sago flour, and feels chewy. If bitten, the sweet taste will be felt.
In that place, two types of Mochi Cakes are sold, namely Mochi Cakes without contents called kiathong and Mochi Cakes filled with peanut mixture. Mochi cake from that place is made without coloring and without preservatives, so it cannot be stored for long, it must be eaten immediately.
